CCNP ENCOR - OSPF Skills

1. OSPF Configuration and Operation

  • OSPFv2: Configure and verify OSPFv2 for IPv4.
  • OSPFv3: Configure and verify OSPFv3 for IPv6.
  • OSPF Router ID: Understand how to set and modify the OSPF Router ID (RID).
  • OSPF Timers: Configure and adjust OSPF timers such as Hello and Dead intervals.
  • OSPF Neighbors: Troubleshoot OSPF neighbor relationships, including authentication and DR/BDR election.
  • OSPF Areas: Configure single-area and multi-area OSPF networks. Understand the concept of Area 0 (backbone area) and non-backbone areas.

2. OSPF Area Types

  • Standard Area: Configure and verify standard OSPF areas.
  • Stub Areas: Configure stub areas to reduce the size of routing tables and limit external route advertisement.
  • Totally Stubby Areas: Configure totally stubby areas to further limit the routing table by restricting external routes and summary routes.
  • Not-So-Stubby Areas (NSSA): Configure NSSA areas, which allow the injection of external routes in a controlled manner.
  • OSPF Autonomous System Boundary Routers (ASBR): Configure ASBRs to handle the redistribution of external routes into OSPF.

3. OSPF Network Types and Topology

  • Broadcast Network: Understand and configure OSPF on Ethernet, FDDI, or other broadcast networks, including the role of DR/BDR.
  • Non-Broadcast Multi-Access (NBMA): Configure OSPF on non-broadcast multi-access (NBMA) networks, such as Frame Relay, and adjust OSPF settings for such environments.
  • Point-to-Point Networks: Configure OSPF on point-to-point networks (such as serial links).
  • OSPF Neighbors on Broadcast and Non-Broadcast Networks: Verify and troubleshoot OSPF neighbor relationships on broadcast and NBMA networks.

4. OSPF Cost and Path Selection

  • Cost Calculation: Understand how OSPF calculates the cost of routes using bandwidth and how to adjust OSPF cost manually to influence path selection.
  • Route Summarization: Implement OSPF route summarization to reduce the size of routing tables, including both manual and automatic summarization.
  • OSPF Load Balancing: Enable and verify OSPF Equal-Cost Multi-Path (ECMP) load balancing.

5. OSPF Authentication

  • OSPF Authentication Types: Configure OSPF MD5 authentication to secure OSPF routing exchanges between routers.

6. OSPF Troubleshooting

  • OSPF Database: Understand and troubleshoot OSPF LSDB (Link-State Database) and LSR (Link-State Request) types.
  • OSPF Neighbor States: Troubleshoot common OSPF neighbor states such as INIT, 2-WAY, EXSTART, EXCHANGE, and FULL.
  • OSPF Show Commands: Use show ip ospf neighbor, show ip ospf, show ip ospf interface, and other related commands to troubleshoot OSPF configurations.
  • OSPF Routing Table: Understand the OSPF routing table, LSAs (Link-State Advertisements), and how OSPF calculates the best path.

7. OSPF Redistribution

  • Redistribution Between OSPF and Other Protocols: Understand how to redistribute routes between OSPF and other routing protocols like EIGRP, BGP, and static routes.
  • Route Filtering: Use route maps, prefix lists, and distribute lists to filter routes during redistribution.

8. OSPF Design and Best Practices

  • OSPF Design Guidelines: Design OSPF networks with optimal area structure, considering factors like number of routers, scalability, and fault tolerance.
  • OSPF Backbone Area: Understand the importance of the backbone area (Area 0) and ensure that all areas connect to it.
